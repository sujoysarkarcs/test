ŚB 10.20.18

Transliteration

dhanur viyati māhendraṁ
nirguṇaṁ ca guṇiny abhāt
vyakte guṇa-vyatikare
’guṇavān puruṣo yathā

Total Translation & Mapping:

When the curved bow 1 of Indra 3[ the rainbow 1] appeared 7 in the sky 2, which had the quality of thundering sound 6, it was unlike ordinary bows 1 because it did not rest upon a string. Similarly, when the Supreme 11 Lord 3 appears in this world, which is the interaction 9 of the material qualities 10, He is unlike ordinary persons 11 because He remains free from all material qualities and independent of all material conditions.

1. dhanuḥ — the bow (rainbow);   2. viyati — within the sky;   3. māhā-indram — of Lord Indra;   4. nirguṇam — without qualities (or without a bowstring);   5. ca — although;   6. guṇini — within the sky, which has definite qualities like sound;   7. abhāt — appeared;   8. vyakte — within the manifest material nature;   9. guṇa-vyatikare — which consists of the interactions of material qualities;   10. aguṇa-vān — He who has no contact with material qualities;   11. puruṣaḥ — the Supreme Personality;   12. yathā — just as.;
